Whether you have a dead gum leaning over your roof, a root system destroying your driveway, or a tree that's simply outgrown your yard — safe tree removal requires proper equipment, planning, and the expertise to manage the unexpected.

Common Questions

Tree Removal FAQs

Do I need council approval to remove a tree in Adelaide?

Possibly. Under South Australia's Planning and Design Code, many trees require Development Approval before they can be removed — particularly those in heritage overlay zones, trees over a certain trunk diameter, or trees listed as significant on your council's register. The rules vary by council area. We assess this during your free quote and handle any required applications on your behalf.

How long does a tree removal take?

Most residential tree removals take between two and six hours, depending on the size of the tree, site access, and whether we're sectioning it into a tight space. Larger trees or those near structures may take a full day. We give you a realistic time estimate during the quote, and we always leave your site clean before we go.

Do you take away all the debris and timber?

Yes — full cleanup and removal of all branches, leaves, and timber is included in every job. We chip smaller material on site. If you have a fireplace or fire pit, we're happy to cut large sections into rounds and leave them for you. Just let us know before we start and we'll organise it on the day.
