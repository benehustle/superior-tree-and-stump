When Storms Hit Adelaide, We Respond

Adelaide is no stranger to severe weather. Summer storms, strong winds, and the occasional downburst can bring down mature trees, snap major limbs, and leave properties in a dangerous state. When that happens, speed and safety go hand in hand.

We handle emergency tree call-outs across Adelaide and surrounding suburbs. Whether a tree has fallen on your roof, is blocking your driveway, or a large limb is hanging precariously over your home, we respond quickly, assess the risk, and work to make the situation safe as a priority, before completing the full removal and cleanup.

For genuinely life-threatening situations involving powerlines or structural damage, your first call should always be to SES on 132 500. Once the immediate risk is managed, call us to deal with the tree itself.

Common Questions

Storm & Emergency FAQs

What should I do immediately after a storm damages a tree?

First, keep everyone away from the tree and any hanging branches, these are unpredictable. If powerlines are involved, keep at least 8 metres away and call SA Power Networks on 13 12 61 immediately. If there's structural damage to your home or a life-threatening hazard, call SES on 132 500. Once the immediate danger is managed, call us on 0431 636 925 and we'll get out to you as quickly as possible.

Are you available for emergency call-outs outside business hours?

We offer emergency call-outs for genuine urgent situations. Call us directly on 0431 636 925 and describe the situation. Our regular hours are Mon–Sat 8:00am–6:00pm, but for serious hazards we do what we can to respond. Emergency work outside standard hours may attract additional charges, which we'll discuss with you clearly before proceeding.

Will my home insurance cover storm tree damage?

In most cases, yes, home and contents insurance in Australia generally covers damage caused by storm events, including fallen trees that damage your home or fences. Check your policy's Product Disclosure Statement for specific inclusions. We can provide written documentation and photos of the damage and work completed, which will support your claim. Contact your insurer as soon as possible to report the event.
